ICAI New Scheme Explained Simply for CA Students

Explanation

Dear future CAs, the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) has introduced a New Scheme of Education and Training, effective from July 1, 2023. For students targeting the CA exams in 2026, this scheme is your primary framework. It aims to create a more skill-centric, globally-aligned, and technologically relevant professional, moving beyond rote learning to emphasize practical application and integrated knowledge.

Here’s a simplified breakdown of the key changes across all levels:

CA Foundation Level:

  • Reduced Papers: The scheme retains 4 papers, but the content has been re-aligned.
  • Focus: Emphasis remains on fundamental concepts, but with a stronger link to application.
  • Negative Marking: Continues for objective-type questions (0.25 marks for each incorrect answer).

CA Intermediate Level:

  • Reduced Papers: The number of papers has been reduced from 8 to 6, structured into two groups of three papers each.
  • Papers Breakdown:
    • Group I: Paper 1: Advanced Accounting, Paper 2: Corporate and Other Laws, Paper 3: Taxation (Income-tax Law & Goods and Services Tax).
    • Group II: Paper 4: Cost and Management Accounting, Paper 5: Auditing and Ethics, Paper 6: Financial Management and Strategic Management.
  • Key Changes: Topics like Information Technology (IT) and Strategic Management (SM) have been either integrated into other papers or moved to the new Self-Paced Online Modules. The focus is on integrated case study-based questions.
  • Self-Paced Online Modules (SPOMs): Intermediate students must clear specific SPOMs before becoming eligible for Final registration, though the primary SPOM requirement is more pronounced for Final.

CA Final Level:

  • Reduced Papers: Like Intermediate, Final papers are reduced from 8 to 6, also in two groups of three papers each.
  • Papers Breakdown:
    • Group I: Paper 1: Financial Reporting, Paper 2: Advanced Financial Management, Paper 3: Advanced Auditing, Assurance and Professional Ethics.
    • Group II: Paper 4: Direct Tax Laws & International Taxation, Paper 5: Indirect Tax Laws, Paper 6: Integrated Business Solutions (Multi-disciplinary Case Study with Strategic Management).
  • Key Changes: Elective papers in the old scheme are now largely covered through mandatory Self-Paced Online Modules. Paper 6 (Integrated Business Solutions) is a game-changer, demanding a holistic, multi-disciplinary approach to case studies.
  • Practical Training (Articleship): Reduced from 3 years to 2 years. You can appear for the Final examination only after completing your 2 years of practical training. There's also a provision for industrial training after the first year of articleship.
  • Self-Paced Online Modules (SPOMs): Mandatory for Final students. You must clear four sets of SPOMs (Set A, B, C, D) through online examinations before being eligible to appear for your Final exams. Sets A (Economics Law) and B (Strategic Cost & Performance Management) are compulsory; Sets C and D are electives.

Overall, the new scheme emphasizes skill development, practical exposure, ethical values, and the integration of technology in the CA profession, ensuring graduates are ready for the evolving demands of the business world.

Examples (Exam-Oriented)

To give you a glimpse into how the examination pattern might shift under the new scheme, here are some conceptual examples:

  1. Intermediate Level - Taxation (Paper 3): Instead of a standalone question asking for the computation of income from a specific head, you might encounter a scenario describing a new business venture. The question could then require you to advise on the most tax-efficient business structure, considering both Income Tax implications (e.g., presumptive taxation, allowable deductions) and Goods and Services Tax (GST) compliance (e.g., registration requirements, input tax credit implications) on the projected revenue and expenditure. This demands an integrated application of both direct and indirect tax laws.

  2. Final Level - Integrated Business Solutions (Paper 6): This paper will be entirely case-study based. Imagine a detailed case study about a listed manufacturing company planning a major overseas expansion. Questions would not be compartmentalized by subject. Instead, you might be asked to prepare a comprehensive report addressing the financial implications (requiring Financial Reporting and Advanced Financial Management knowledge), audit considerations (Advanced Auditing), tax planning strategies (Direct and Indirect Tax Laws), and ethical dilemmas or regulatory compliance issues arising from the expansion. Your answer needs to weave together concepts from various subjects to provide a holistic business solution.

  3. Intermediate Level - Auditing & Ethics (Paper 5): Rather than theoretical questions like "List the types of audit evidence," you might be presented with an extract of a company's internal control system for its inventory management or a snippet from an auditor's working paper showing identified discrepancies. The question would then ask you to identify control weaknesses, suggest specific audit procedures to address the risk, and discuss any ethical threats an auditor might face while reporting on such findings, along with safeguards. This tests your practical judgment and ethical reasoning.

  4. Application-Based Questions Across Levels: Expect a shift from direct recall to scenario-based problem-solving. For instance, in Cost & Management Accounting, instead of simply calculating variances, a question might present a company struggling with profitability for a specific product and ask you to recommend cost control measures or pricing strategies based on marginal costing or activity-based costing principles, justifying your recommendations with calculations and qualitative arguments.

FAQs

  • Question: When does the new scheme effectively apply to students?
    Answer: The new scheme became effective from July 1, 2023, for all new registrations. While the first exams under this scheme for Foundation and Intermediate were held in May 2024, for students targeting the 2026 examinations, you will entirely be under the new scheme from the beginning of your journey.
  • Question: What are Self-Paced Online Modules (SPOMs) and how important are they?
    Answer: SPOMs are mandatory online learning modules that you need to complete and clear through online exams. There are four sets (A, B, C, D) for the Final level, with Set A (Economics Law) and Set B (Strategic Cost & Performance Management) being compulsory. Sets C and D are electives. You must clear these SPOMs before you can appear for your CA Final examinations. They are designed to cover broader knowledge areas relevant to a CA professional.
  • Question: How has the practical training (articleship) duration changed under the new scheme?
    Answer: The articleship duration has been significantly reduced from 3 years to 2 years. A key change is that you become eligible to appear for the CA Final examinations only after completing your entire 2 years of practical training. There is also an option for industrial training after the first year of articleship.
  • Question: Is there negative marking in all papers under the new scheme?
    Answer: Negative marking (0.25 marks for each incorrect answer) continues for the objective-type questions at the Foundation level. For Intermediate and Final levels, while the papers are primarily subjective, some may include objective-type or case study-based MCQs where specific instructions regarding negative marking will be provided by ICAI in the exam guidelines for each paper.
  • Question: Will the passing percentage criteria change for individual papers or aggregate?
    Answer: The fundamental passing criteria remain consistent: a minimum of 40% marks in each individual paper and an aggregate of 50% marks for the group. However, with the reduced number of papers and the increased focus on integrated and application-based questions, your study strategy will need to be more holistic and conceptual to meet these criteria effectively.

Call-to-Action

The new ICAI scheme is a strategic step towards enhancing the quality and relevance of the CA qualification. To excel in this updated environment, a robust and adaptable study plan is crucial. Focus on conceptual clarity rather than mere memorization. Develop strong analytical and problem-solving skills, especially for integrated case studies. Regularly engage with the latest ICAI Study Material, which is aligned with the new syllabus. For your revision plan, prioritize understanding the interconnectedness of topics across subjects. Make it a point to practice extensively using the Revised Test Papers (RTPs) and Mock Test Papers (MTPs) released by ICAI for the new scheme, as these will provide the most accurate insights into the expected question patterns and difficulty levels. While previous year's questions (PYQs) can offer valuable practice for fundamental concepts, always cross-reference with the new syllabus to ensure relevance, as certain topics and question styles have evolved. Embrace the change, study smart, and your success in 2026 will be well within reach!
