CA Inter Costing Most Repeated PYQs (Last 10 Attempts Analysis)

Explanation

As aspiring Chartered Accountants preparing for the May 2026 CA Inter exams, understanding the pulse of the examiner is crucial. While ICAI rarely repeats exact questions, certain *concepts*, *question patterns*, and *problem types* consistently appear across attempts. An analysis of the last 10 attempts in Costing reveals a clear inclination towards testing foundational concepts with practical application. The frequently tested areas primarily revolve around core costing methods and techniques. Key repeated themes emerge from chapters like Standard Costing (variance analysis), Marginal Costing (decision-making problems, break-even analysis), Budgetary Control (flexible budget, cash budget), Process Costing (equivalent production, inter-process transfers), and Activity-Based Costing (ABC). Questions from Material Costing (EOQ, inventory control techniques) and Labour Costing (incentive schemes, labour turnover) also feature regularly, often as smaller sub-parts or direct questions. The examiners tend to focus on your ability to apply costing principles to various business scenarios, interpret results, and provide reasoned conclusions. This deep understanding, rather than rote memorization, is what will help you tackle the most repeated question patterns successfully.

Examples (Exam-Oriented)

Here are common patterns and how to approach them:

1. Comprehensive Variance Analysis Problem: Questions frequently require calculating various variances (material price, usage, labour rate, efficiency, variable overhead expenditure, efficiency, fixed overhead volume, expenditure) and then preparing a reconciliation statement (e.g., between budgeted and actual profit). The key is to meticulously draw up standard cost and actual cost sheets, clearly showing working notes for each variance calculation, and ensure the reconciliation statement balances perfectly. Focus on understanding the *cause* of each variance rather than just the formula.

2. Marginal Costing Decision-Making Scenarios: These problems often present a business situation (e.g., make or buy, accept/reject a special order, optimize product mix under a limiting factor, shut down a division, drop a product line) and ask you to advise management. The approach should be to identify relevant costs and revenues, calculate contribution per unit or per limiting factor, and base your decision solely on incremental analysis, ignoring fixed costs that are not differential. Clearly state assumptions and provide a concise recommendation.

3. Process Costing with Equivalent Production: A recurring question type involves preparing process accounts, often with scenarios like normal loss, abnormal loss, abnormal gain, and inter-process transfers (sometimes with profit loading). You'll need to calculate equivalent units of production (using FIFO or Weighted Average method, as specified), determine cost per equivalent unit, and value output, abnormal loss/gain, and closing WIP. Attention to detail in calculating equivalent units and proper valuation is paramount.

4. Budgetary Control and Cost Sheets: Expect questions on preparing flexible budgets for different activity levels, cash budgets for a specific period, or operational budgets (e.g., production, raw material purchase). Additionally, preparing a detailed Cost Sheet or Statement of Cost of Production, identifying Prime Cost, Works Cost, Cost of Production, Cost of Sales, and Profit, is a staple. Ensure you correctly classify costs (direct/indirect, fixed/variable) and handle opening/closing stocks appropriately.
